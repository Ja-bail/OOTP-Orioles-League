Osterhouse Selected MLBDL's Top Player
Thursday, August 13th , 2026
The future looks extremely bright for Justin Osterhouse. At the age of 21, many players are still trying to find their way in MLB Draft League.

Osterhouse has not only found his way, he's already established himself as one of the game's brightest stars, today being named the winner of the MLB Draft League Most Valuable Player.

"I didn't expect to hit .299 this year, that's for sure," the center fielder said.

The Aberdeen IronBirds star also put up a .368 on-base percentage this year, with 66 hits, 7 doubles, no triples, 11 home runs, 33 RBIs and 24 runs scored.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 6. Kolby Branch of the Mahoning Valley Scrappers finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Jonathan Kim of the Williamsport Crosscutters finished third.
