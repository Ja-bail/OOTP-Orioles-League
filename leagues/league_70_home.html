#1 Player Named by Arizona Fall League
Friday, November 20th , 2026
The future looks extremely bright for Joseph Sullivan. At the age of 24, many players are still trying to find their way in Arizona Fall League.

Sullivan has not only found his way, he's already established himself as one of the game's brightest stars, today being named the winner of the Arizona Fall League Most Valuable Player.

"I didn't expect to hit .299 this year, that's for sure," the right fielder said.

The Glendale Desert Dogs star also put up a .382 on-base percentage this year, with 29 hits, 3 doubles, no triples, 9 home runs, 15 RBIs and 18 runs scored.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 6. Josue De Paula of the Glendale Desert Dogs finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Sean Keys of the Mesa Solar Sox finished third.
